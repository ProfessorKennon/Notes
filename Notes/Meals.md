**Recipe for Birria Tacos**

**Ingredients:**

*For the Birria:*
- 2 lbs beef chuck roast or short ribs, cut into chunks
- 4 dried guajillo chiles, seeds removed
- 2 dried ancho chiles, seeds removed
- 1 chipotle pepper in adobo sauce (optional for heat)
- 4 large tomatoes, chopped
- 1 white onion, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 cups beef broth
- 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil

*For the Tacos:*
- Corn tortillas
- 1 cup chopped white onion
- 1 cup chopped fresh cilantro
- Lime wedges
- 1 cup shredded mozzarella or Oaxaca cheese

**Instructions:**
1. **Prepare the Chiles:** In a skillet over medium heat, toast the guajillo and ancho chiles until fragrant. Transfer to a bowl and cover with hot water. Let them soak for about 15 minutes until softened.
2. **Blend the Sauce:** Add the soaked chiles, chipotle pepper, tomatoes, onion, garlic, beef broth, vinegar, oregano, cumin, paprika, and a pinch of salt and pepper to a blender. Blend until smooth.
3. **Cook the Birria:** In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef pieces and sear until browned on all sides. Pour the blended sauce over the beef and bring to a simmer. Lower the heat, cover, and let it cook for about 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the beef is tender and shreds easily.
4. **Assemble the Tacos:** Shred the beef and return it to the sauce. Heat a separate skillet over medium heat. Dip each tortilla into the sauce, ensuring both sides are coated, then place on the skillet. Add shredded beef, cheese, and fold the tortilla over. Cook until crispy.
5. **Serve:** Serve tacos with chopped onion, cilantro, and lime wedges. Enjoy your birria tacos with a side of the consomé (the sauce) for dipping.

Feel free to adjust spice levels and add your favorite toppings to make it your own!

**Olive Garden Alfredo Sauce Recipe**

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter
- 2 cups heavy cream
- 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
- Fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

**Instructions:**
1. **Melt the Butter:**
2. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Be careful not to let it burn.
3. **Add the Cream:**
4. Once the butter is melted, slowly add the heavy cream to the saucepan. Stir continuously with a whisk to combine the butter and cream.
5. **Simmer the Sauce:**
6. Allow the mixture to simmer for about 5-7 minutes, stirring frequently. Do not let it come to a full boil.
7. **Incorporate the Cheese:**
8. Gradually add the grated Parmesan cheese to the sauce, stirring constantly until the cheese is fully melted and the sauce is smooth and creamy.
9. **Season the Sauce:**
10. Season the Alfredo sauce with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Stir well to combine.
11. **Serve:**
12. Remove the sauce from heat and serve it immediately over your choice of pasta. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired.

**Tips:**
- For a richer flavor, you can add a clove of minced garlic to the butter as it melts.
- Ensure your Parmesan cheese is freshly grated for the best texture and flavor.
- Customize your Alfredo by adding cooked chicken, shrimp, or vegetables.

Enjoy your homemade Olive Garden-style Alfredo sauce with your favorite pasta dish!
