# Tabernacle Prayer



- &nbsp;
	1. **Thanksgiving:** all the blessings in your life
	1. **Repentance:** acknowledge Jesus sacrifice and confess
	2. **Cleansing:** ask God to purify your heart and mind.  Mind/Heart of Jesus, covenant eyes, consecrate my tongue, hands do work for you.
	3. **Spirit Empowerment:**
		1. **The Spirit of the LORD:** The foundational presence and authority of God.
		2. **The Spirit of Wisdom:** The ability to perceive and apply God’s truth to life. 
		3. **The Spirit of Understanding:** The ability to discern and comprehend spiritual reality.  
		4. **The Spirit of Counsel:** The ability to guide, advise, and make godly decisions.  
		5. **The Spirit of Might:** Supernatural strength, power, or dunamis to act. 
		6. **The Spirit of Knowledge:** An intimate, experiential knowledge of God.  
		7. **The Spirit of the Fear of the LORD:** A holy reverence, awe, and submission to God.
		8. Or Fruits of the Spirits:
			1. **Love** (Agape): Unconditional, selfless love for others.
			2. **Joy** (Chara): A deep-seated gladness that is not dependent on external circumstances.
			3. **Peace** (Eirene): A sense of inner tranquility and wholeness, even amidst turmoil.
			4. **Patience** (Makrothumia): The ability to endure difficult situations or people without retaliating or giving up.
			5. **Kindness** (Chrestotes): A gentle, helpful, and gracious disposition toward others.
			6. **Goodness** (Agathosune): Virtue in action; acting with integrity and generosity.
			7. **Faithfulness** (Pistis): Loyalty, reliability, and steadfastness in commitments.
			8. **Gentleness** (Prautes): Strength under control; a humble and submissive spirit.
			9. **Self-Control** (Egkrateia): The ability to restrain passions, appetites, and impulses.
	5. **Word:** read the Bible
	6. **Worship:** sing a song
	7. **Intercession:** praying for others



	**Thanksgiving:** Shifting your focus from yourself to God by listing things you are grateful for (the "Gate" of the tabernacle).
	• **Repentance:** A time of "Brazen Altar" honesty, acknowledging where you have missed the mark.
	• **Cleansing:** A prayer of "Laver" renewal, asking God to wash your heart and mind for the day.
	• **Spirit Empowerment:** Asking the Holy Spirit to fill you, give you wisdom, and lead you in your decisions.
	• **Word:** Meditating on specific scriptures to align your thinking with God’s truth.
	• **Worship:** Moving into the "Altar of Incense" to praise God simply for who He is.
	• **Intercession:** Ending with the "Ark of the Covenant" by presenting the needs of others and your own burdens to the Father.

	1. **Thanksgiving:** Entering into prayer by expressing gratitude.  
	2. **Repentance:** Acknowledging and confessing sins.  
	3. **Cleansing:** Asking for purification and spiritual renewal.  
	4. **Spirit Empowerment:** Praying for the filling and guidance of the Holy Spirit.  
	5. **Word:** Reading or meditating on Scripture.  
	6. **Worship:** Focusing on God’s character and worthiness.
	7. **Intercession:** Lifting up the needs of others, the church, and the community.  

**1. The Outer Court**
• **The Gate (Thanksgiving and Praise):** Just as the Israelites entered the Tabernacle through the gate, you begin your prayer by acknowledging God’s presence with gratitude and praise. (Psalm 100:4)
• **The Brazen Altar (Confession):** This represents the cross of Jesus. It is a place to bring your sins, repent, and acknowledge the sacrifice made for your redemption.
• **The Laver (Cleansing and Renewal):** A place for washing and reflection. You pray for God to reveal and cleanse areas of your heart and mind, asking for purification and renewal to walk in His ways.
**2. The Holy Place**
• **The Golden Lampstand (Guidance and the Holy Spirit):** This represents the light of the Holy Spirit. You pray for the Spirit to illuminate your life, guide your path, and provide wisdom, counsel, and understanding.
• **The Table of Showbread (The Word of God):** This represents dependence on God’s Word for daily sustenance. You pray for a fresh word from God, revelation, and the application of Scripture to your life and for the needs of others.
• **The Altar of Incense (Worship and Intercession):** The fragrance of incense rising to God represents worship and prayer. Here, you focus on the character of God, honor His names, and offer intercessory prayers for others (the church, leadership, family, etc.).
**3. The Holy of Holies**
• **The Ark of the Covenant / Mercy Seat (The Presence of God):** This is the destination of the prayer journey. Having moved through the previous stages, you enter the "throne room" with confidence. This is where you bring your deepest petitions and requests, resting in the assurance of God’s presence, mercy, and righteousness.
