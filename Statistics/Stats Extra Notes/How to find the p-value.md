# How to find the p-value



## Intro:
There are 3 options and you have to think about what you are doing.  You are finding the area under the curve to the left, to the right, or both sides.  Below you will see how to find each of them.  To figure out which one to use, you have to use the problem, the key words to look for are highlighted green below.
## 
Less Than:
If the problem says  <span style="background-color: #00C6BD;">
     less than or below
 </span> or anything that makes you shade  <span style="background-color: #AF52DD;">
     TO THE LEFT
 </span>, just insert test statistic into the ALEKS calculator.
![D07E1468-8FD5-461F-ADDC-C838CAAE34CE](images/D07E1468-8FD5-461F-ADDC-C838CAAE34CE.heic)



## Greater Than:
If the problem says  <span style="background-color: #00C6BD;">
     greater than or more than or above
 </span> or anything that makes you shade  <span style="background-color: #AF52DD;">
     TO THE RIGHT
 </span>, you have to do 1 - P(z<) in the calculator.  This essentially “flips” the sign.  What you are actually doing below is finding the area to the left of 1.64 which is .9595 and then doing 1 - .9595 to get .0505.  Or easy terms: to the left of 1.64 is 95% shaded, but we want the right so that would be 100% - 95% ￼.

## Different:
If the problem says  <span style="background-color: #00C6BD;">
     not equal to or different
 </span> or anything that creates  <span style="background-color: #AF52DD;">
     TWO TAILS
 </span>, as in, you want a number that could be less than the null or greater than the hull either way is fine, then you have a 2 tail test.  So you will do P(z< ) times 2.  The reason you do this is because the whole thing is symmetric.  So you find the area to the left and then double it (or to the right and then double it)

The trick here with ALEKS is that the explanations switches which tail you are finding and then doubling.  Sometimes it will do the left tail (the negative test statistic) and then double it.  Sometimes it will do the right tail (the positive test statistic) so you have to do 1 - P(z<) then double it.  

**BUT I recommend ALWAYS using the left tail (negative test statistic) and doubling it because it’s easier and you don’t have to worry about doing one minus.**
